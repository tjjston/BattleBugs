🏷️ Tier System — How Bugs Are Ranked

Every bug is assigned to one of six tiers based on its total combat footprint. Tier is determined by the higher of two paths: (1) the sum of all six stats (attack, defense, speed, lethality, grip, cunning), or (2) a "danger floor" that elevates a bug when it has class-leading lethality — so a small but deadly spider isn't trapped in a low tier just because her body is fragile.

Tier Power Range What it means Examples
UBER
Legendary		 540-600 Rare, apex combat threats. Either physical apex predator OR class-leading lethality + supporting trait. Asian giant hornet, large emperor scorpion, goliath beetle, Sydney funnel-web spider, Brazilian wandering spider, deathstalker scorpion
OU
Overused		 480-539 Top-tier hunters, or single-dimension class-leading killers (small body + extreme venom). Tarantula, wolf spider, large stag beetle, brown recluse, black widow, tarantula hawk wasp, Indian red scorpion
UU
Underused		 400-479 Capable adults — solid generalists, large beetles, mid-size mantises, jumping spiders. Paper wasp, large grasshopper, orb weaver, mid-size mantis, Portia jumping spider, velvet ant, bombardier beetle
RU
Rarely Used		 320-399 Common adults of moderate ability. Predators with a single strong dimension. Bumblebee, garden spider, June beetle, tiger beetle, pseudoscorpion, honeybee worker
NU
Never Used		 240-319 Small, short-lived, no signature weaponry. The "garden bug" middle. Ladybug, common ant worker, small moth, fruit fly, lacewing, leafhopper, pill bug, stink bug
ZU
Zero Used		 0-239 Fragile or microscopic. Often pre-adult or extremely specialized scavengers. Aphid, springtail, mite, soft larva, gnat, mosquito larva
🪲 Why might a bug be in multiple tiers?

Some bugs qualify for two tiers at once and the system takes the higher of the two:

  • Brown recluse — ~8mm body, fragile, slow. By stat sum she lands in NU. But necrotic venom is class-leading, so the danger floor pulls her to OU. Final tier: OU.
  • Black widow — small + delicate, but α-latrotoxin is among the most potent arachnid venoms. Sum says NU; floor says OU. OU.
  • Sydney funnel-web — venom + aggression + repeated bites. Triggers Uber-level danger floor even if her stat sum lands in UU. Uber.
  • Goliath beetle — no venom, but mass + crushing jaws push her sum into OU/Uber territory without any danger floor needed. Uber by sum alone.
⚖️ Two principles to remember

1. Mass bounds force. Body size limits attack, defense, and grip (mass-bound stats). A 10mm bug can't punch like a 50mm bug, no matter how hard its shell looks.

2. Biology bounds danger. Speed, lethality, and cunning are not size-capped. A 10mm spider with neurotoxin can drop a target 100× her size; a 6mm Portia jumping spider out-thinks bugs twice her age.

This is why ladybugs sit in NU (no venom, no signature defense → nothing earns elevation) while brown recluses sit in OU (necrotic venom earns the floor).

Danger Floor cheat sheet: lethality ≥ 95 and cunning ≥ 75 → Uber · lethality ≥ 90 → OU · lethality ≥ 80 → UU · lethality ≥ 65 or cunning ≥ 80 → RU
